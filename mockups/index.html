Five ideas for the hero panel

Today the hero shows an "ANP readiness map" with bars filled to made-up levels — it looks like a score but doesn't measure anything. Below are five ways to make that space actually useful. Each one is clickable and interactive. Open them, try them, and tell us which direction(s) you like.

Tip for reviewers: these are quick prototypes of just the panel, shown in a stand-in hero. Wording and exact items are placeholders we can tune.
1

Readiness self-check

Tick real yes/no items and the bars fill for a live readiness score you can print or share. Makes the "readiness" name honest.

Interactive checklist · saves your answers 2

Animated join sequence

A looping micro-animation of how a device joins: Beacon → ANQP → Match → EAP → RadSec. Teaches the whole flow at a glance.

Eye-catching · explanatory 3

"Where should you start?" router

Pick your use case (carrier offload, venue, enterprise, OpenRoaming) and the panel highlights what matters and links you to the right sections.

Personalized on-ramp 4

Acronym launchpad + progress

Every chip explains itself on hover and links to the glossary; bars fill as you explore the matching sections. Reuses what we just built.

Low effort · ties into glossary 5

Maturity slider

Slide from R1 → R2 → R3 → OpenRoaming and each pillar shows what you need at that level. Great for "what do I add to level up?"

Interactive · shows the path
